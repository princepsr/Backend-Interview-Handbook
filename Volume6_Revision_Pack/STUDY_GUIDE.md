# Volume 6: Revision Pack — How to Use It

## What This Volume Is

Vol 6 is NOT for learning new material. It is for final revision and interview simulation. Every chapter is a compressed extract of the core volume it maps to. Use it after completing the relevant core volume — reading it cold without the foundation will not stick.

## The Night-Before Protocol (2 hours)

Use this the evening before your interview — no new learning, only reinforcement.

**Hour 1 — Topic-specific revision (90 min)**
1. Identify which 1–2 topics the interview will test heaviest (Java internals? Spring? System design?).
2. Open the matching revision chapter. Read Q&As in this order: one-liner first, pause, then read the full answer.
3. Flag any Q&A where the one-liner felt incomplete. Re-read only those full answers.
4. Stop after 60 minutes of active reading.

**Final 30 min — Speak aloud**
1. Pick 10 Q&As you flagged as shaky.
2. Close the book. Say the one-liner out loud.
3. Speak the full answer as if to an interviewer — complete sentences, not bullet fragments.
4. This is the single most effective retention technique. Do not skip it.

**Do NOT**: read new chapters, watch new videos, or start a new topic after 9 PM the night before.

---

## Morning-Of Protocol (30 minutes)

**Read fully (15 min)**: Ch27 Q1–Q10 (HLD top 5 designs) and the one-liner answers for Ch23 / Ch24 depending on the role.

**Skim (10 min)**: The "Common Mistakes" section of whichever volume is most relevant. These are the things that lose points — keep them front of mind.

**Skip entirely**: Any topic you have not studied. Do not try to learn something new in the 30 minutes before an interview.

**Last 5 min**: Write on paper (or type) the 7-step system design framework. The physical act of writing it reactivates memory better than reading it.

## Self-Test Method

Turn any Vol 6 Q&A into an active self-test in 4 steps:

1. **Cover** the full answer — only the question and one-liner are visible.
2. **Say the one-liner** out loud from memory. If you cannot recall it, glance once, then look away.
3. **Speak the full answer** aloud in 60–90 seconds as if you are in the interview. Complete sentences. Include a concrete example or a trade-off.
4. **Score yourself**: full marks if you covered the key point and an example; partial if you only stated the definition; zero if you needed to read the answer to speak it.

Target: 8 out of 10 Q&As at full marks before you walk into the interview. If you are scoring 5 or below, go back to the core volume — the revision chapter alone will not fill a genuine knowledge gap.
